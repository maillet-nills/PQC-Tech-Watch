## Description
**Practical article covering how quantum computing threatens modern cryptography**. It explains why qubits (superposition + entanglement) allow quantum computers to break the mathematical foundations of [[RSA]], [[ECC]], and [[Diffie-Hellman]]. 

The **two key algorithms** covered are [[Shor's Algorithm]] (breaks asymmetric encryption) and [[Grover's Algorithm]] (weakens symmetric encryption).

## Why it's relevant
**It lists 8 concrete risks :**

- Breaking public-key encryption
- [["Harvest Now, Decrypt Later"]]
- Compromising digital signatures
- Undermining blockchain integrity
- Weakening secure communications
- Exposing IoT ecosystems
- Endangering critical infrastructure
- Creating geopolitical imbalance
