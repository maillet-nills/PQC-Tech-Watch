## Summary

Reuters interview with Wang Xiaoyun (Tsinghua University), conducted on the sidelines of China's National People's Congress.

China is expected to release its own national PQC standards within 3 years, independently of NIST and based on **structure-less lattice** algorithms like S-Cloud+, which Chinese cryptographers believe are more secure than the **algebraic lattice** designs underpinning ML-KEM and ML-DSA.

## Key Points
- Wang Xiaoyun predicts a **"3-to-5 year explosive growth"** window for PQC industry migration in China.
- China issued a **global call for algorithm proposals** in 2025, no selection announced yet. National GB/T standards are not yet published.
- Chinese researchers chose a **different mathematical approach** from NIST : structure-less lattices (e.g. S-Cloud+) vs. algebraic lattices (ML-KEM / ML-DSA). Wang argues algebraic lattices "have some degree of security degradation" that structure-less designs do not.
- Priority sectors for migration: **finance and energy**, given data sensitivity.
- China's new **five-year plan** elevated quantum technology to a core strategic industry, alongside embodied AI, nuclear fusion and brain-computer interfaces.
- Same week: Trump administration pledged to **sustain US dominance** in PQC and AI. South Korea announced its own PQC migration plan targeting full industry coverage by 2035.
